# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People trying to understand Egyptian public data: citizens, journalists, researchers, civic technologists, and policy-minded users. They arrive with concrete questions about government, debt, budget, institutions, sources, and public investment context, often needing a quick but trustworthy answer.

## Product Purpose

Mizan makes Egypt's government and public data visible through sourced data pages, tools, and an AI-assisted generative interface. Success means users can inspect a claim, see the source behind it, understand the confidence level, and move between explanation, data, and tools without losing trust.

## Brand Personality

Clear, accountable, and utilitarian. The product should feel precise and calm rather than decorative or promotional.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-site hero patterns, decorative dashboard cards, arbitrary AI-generated layouts, hidden provenance, unsourced numbers, and chat experiences that feel like disconnected workflow templates. Avoid dense mobile layouts that force horizontal scrolling or make controls unusable.

## Design Principles

1. Show the evidence with the answer.
2. Keep generated UI structured, not arbitrary.
3. Prioritize readable data over decorative composition.
4. Preserve context across chat turns.
5. Make every core workflow work on touch and narrow screens.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible controls. Support Arabic and English, RTL and LTR layout, reduced motion, touch targets of at least 44px, and responsive layouts that do not rely on hover or horizontal scrolling for core functionality.
